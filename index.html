How Sage Intacct punchout works

One integration. Buyers shop on the supplier's website, and clean order data flows end to end.

Punch out

The buyer clicks through from Sage Intacct into the supplier's webstore — already authenticated, with contract pricing applied.

Shop

They browse the supplier's live catalogue and fill a basket exactly as they would on any e-commerce site.

Return the basket

At checkout, the basket returns to Sage Intacct as a draft purchase order — line items, pricing and coding intact.

Approve & transact

The PO follows your normal Intacct approval workflow, then transmits to the supplier automatically. Invoices flow back for matching.

Sage Intacct punchout FAQs

Common questions about connecting Sage Intacct to supplier webstores.

What is Sage Intacct punchout?

Punchout lets a buyer click out from Sage Intacct purchasing into a supplier's webstore, shop the supplier's live catalogue, and return the basket to Sage Intacct as a draft purchase order. The PO then follows your normal Intacct approval workflow and is transmitted to the supplier automatically.

What do suppliers need to support Sage Intacct punchout?

The supplier needs a webstore that supports punchout integration (for example cXML or OCI). punchout.ws provides the middleware that connects the supplier webstore to Sage Intacct and manages the document flow between them.

How much does a Sage Intacct punchout integration cost?

Sage Intacct customers pay a one-off setup fee per supplier connection, with no ongoing charges. Suppliers pay a 3% fee on transactions processed through the service, with no setup cost.
